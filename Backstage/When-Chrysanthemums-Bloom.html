In late spring, when chrysanthemums bloom, grief hits.

I barely knew him. Thoughts and memories scatter, forgotten, like dandelion seeds dancing along a windy breeze.

I chase those dandelion seeds.

The regrets cloud the mind. Though the days and months pass, these emotions stand still.

Pictures I do not have, your voice I remember not, I wonder where I find you?

August changed. Years have passed, and more will do so.

Time never heals all.

Even when chrysanthemums have withered, still, it stays.

In late spring, when chrysanthemums bloom again, grief remains.
