Stoichiometry comes from two Greek words: stoicheion (element) and metron (measure). As Jeremias Richter wrote in 1792, "Stoichiometry is the science of measuring the quantitative proportions or mass ratios in which chemical elements stand to one another."

The Mole

The mole (symbol mol) is one of the most important units in chemistry. It is a unit of measurement for an amount of substance. $$1\ \text{mol} = 6.022 \times 10^{23}\ \text{entities} \tag{1}$$ The number 6.022 × 1023 mol-1 is called Avogadro's number. It is often denoted as \(N_A\).
How many atoms are present in 2.14 moles of Vanadium?
We can use \(N_A\) to convert moles into atoms. $$2.14\ \text{mol V} = \frac{6.022 \times 10^{23}\ \text{atoms V}}{1\ \text{mol V}} = 2.1077 \times 10^{23}\ \text{atoms V}$
