Intermolecular Forces

There are four types of intermolecular forces:

  • hydrogen bonding
  • dispersion forces
  • dipole-dipole attractions
  • ion-dipole attractions

Hydrogen Bonding

A hydrogen bond is a special type of intermolecular attraction between hydrogen and other more electronegative atoms (fluorine, oxygen, nitrogen). It is the strongest intermolecular force out of the four.

Some examples include hydrogen fluoride (HF), ammonia (NH3), and hydrogen sulfide (H2S).

Dispersion Forces

Dispersion forces (also known as London dispersion forces) are temporarily created when two adjacent atoms form dipoles. It is present in all molecules, and is the weakest type of intermolecular force.

London dispersion forces are the only forces in nonpolar molecules such as CH4 and Br2.
