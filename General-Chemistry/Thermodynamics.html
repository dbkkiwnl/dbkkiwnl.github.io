Thermodynamics (or chemical thermodynamics) is the study of the interrelation of heat and work with chemical reactions or with physical changes of state within the confines of the laws of thermodynamics.

Some changes seem to happen in one direction but not the other. For example, a fuel burns in air and forms CO2 and H2O, but those products will never react to re-form the fuel and oxygen. This is an example of spontaneous change.

The Second Law of Thermodynamics

The disorder or entropy (S) of the universe is spontaneous.

$$S_\text{universe} = S_\text{system} + S_\text{surroundings} \tag{1}$$

As such, when Suniverse > 0, it is a spontaneous process.

Standard Enthalpy of Reaction (ΔSo rxn)

The entropy change that occurs when all reactands and products are in their standard state. If the number of moles of gas increases, the standard enthalpy is positive. If the number decreases, it is negative.

$$\Delta S^{\text{o}}\ \text{rxn} = \sum mS^{\text{o}}\ \text{(product)} - \sum nS^{\text{o}}\ \text{(reactant)} \tag{2}$$
