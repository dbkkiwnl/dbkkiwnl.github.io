General Chemistry

Electrochemistry

Thermodynamics has countless applications. Some that are within your reach like a laptop or a tablet. These are everyday objects that rely on applied thermodynamics called electrochemistry.

Reduction-oxidation reaction

Reduction-oxidation reactions (or redox reactions) include the formation of a compound from its elements (and the reverse process), all combustion reactions, the generation of electricity in batteries, the production of cellular energy, and many others.

  • Oxidation is the loss of electrons.
  • Reduction is the gain of electrons.

Each atom in a molecule (or formula unit) is assigned an oxidation number (ON). An oxidation number has the sign before the number (as in +2), whereas a charge has the sign after the number (as in 2+).

Rules for Assigning an Oxidation Number
  1. For an atom in its elemental form (Na, O2, Cl2, etc.): ON = 0
  2. For a monatomic ion: ON = charge
  3. For Group 1: ON = +1
  4. For Group 2: ON = +2
  5. For hydrogen: ON = +1 (with nonmetals); ON = -1 (with metals and boron)
  6. For fluorine: ON = -1
  7. For oxygen: ON = -1 (in peroxides); ON = -2 (all other compounds)
  8. For group 7: ON = -1
