Reaction Rate

The speed of a chemical reaction, or its rate, is defined as:

$$\text{rate} = \frac{\Delta \text{concentration}}{\Delta \text{time}} \tag{1}$$

For a reaction in the form \(\ce{A + B -> C}\), the rate can be expressed as:

$$\text{rate} = -\frac{\Delta [A]}{\Delta t} = -\frac{\Delta [B]}{\Delta t} = \frac{\Delta [C]}{\Delta t} \tag{2}$$

Observe that the signs for the reactants A and B are negative, while the product C is positive. For products and reactants with numerical coefficients other than 1, the reaction rate is expressed a little differently. For example,

$$\ce{H2 (g) + I2 (g) -> 2HI (g)}$$

The rate of the equation above is:

$$\text{rate} = -\frac{\Delta \ce{[H2]}}{\Delta t} = -\frac{\Delta \ce{[I2]}}{\Delta t} = \frac{\Delta \ce{[HI]}}{2 \Delta t}$$

For HI's numerical coefficient 2, it is expressed in its reciprocal form 1/2. This is how numerical coefficients are written in reaction rate.

Hydrogen gas has a nonpolluting combustion product (water vapor). It is used as a fuel aboard the space shuttle and in earthbound cars with prototype engines. $$\ce{2H2 (g) + O2 (g) -> 2H2O (g)}$$ (a) Express the rate in terms of changes in [H2], [O2], [H2O] with time. (b) When [O2] is decreasing at 0.23 mol/L⋅s, at what rate is [H2O] increasing?
Show solution
(a) Referring to Equation 1, $$\text{rate} = -\frac{\Delta \ce{[H2]}}{2 \Delta t} = -\frac{\Delta \ce{[O2]}}{\Delta t} = \frac{\Delta \ce{[H2O]}}{2 \Delta t}$$
