Problem Set: Intermolecular Forces

Part I. Determine the strongest intermolecular force present.

  1. HF - hydrogen bonding
  2. H2O - hydrogen bonding
  3. NO - dipole-dipole attraction
  4. CH3Cl - dipole-dipole attraction
  5. HCl - dipole-dipole attraction
  6. K2S - ion-dipole attraction
  7. I2 - London dispersion forces
  8. CO - dipole-dipole attraction
  9. F2 - London dispersion forces
  10. SO2 - dipole-dipole attraction

Part II. Identify all intermolecular forces present.

  1. CH3CF3 - London dispersion, dipole-dipole
  2. CCl4 - dipole-dipole
  3. SO2 - dipole-dipole
  4. BrF - dipole-dipole
  5. (CH3)3N - dispersion, dipole-dipole
  6. PCl5 - dipole-dipole

Part III. For each given substance, identify the strongest intermolecular force present, then rank each one relative to each other (1 for strongest, 3 for weakest).

  • HBr - dipole-dipole attraction (2)
  • O2 - London dispersion forces (3)
  • CH3OH - hydrogen bonding (1)
