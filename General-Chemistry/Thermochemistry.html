Thermochemistry

Thermochemistry is the study of heat energy that is associated with chemical reactions and phase changes.

Energy

Energy is defined as the capacity to do work or transfer heat. There are two types. Kinetic energy is the energy an object possesses due to its motion. Potential energy is the energy "at rest"â€”having the potential to become another form of energy.

Work is the energy used to cause an object to move against a force, and heat is the energy used to cause the temperature of an object to increase.

Units of Energy

  • A joule is the amount of energy needed to move a 1 kilogram mass a distance of 1 meter.
    • $$ \text{1 J} = \frac{\text{1 kg} \cdot m^2}{s^2} \tag{1}$$
  • A calorie (different from Calorie, in food), is defined as:
    • $$ \text{1 cal} = \text{4.184 J} \tag{2}$$

Systems and Surroundings

It is important to define a system and its surroundings in any thermodynamic study. A system is what is being focused on, and everything else is the surroundings.

Energy Transfer

Heat (\(q\)) is the energy transferred as a result of a difference in temperature between a system and its surroundings.

Work (\(w\)) is the energy transferred when an object is moved by force.
