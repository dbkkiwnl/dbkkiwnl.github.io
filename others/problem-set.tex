\begin{problem}
    A system receives \SI{425}{J} of heat from and delivers \SI{425}{J} of work to its surroundings. What is the change in internal energy of the system in J?

    \textit{Solution.} Change in internal energy $\Delta E$ is expressed as $q + w$.

    Given:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item $q = \SI{425}{J}$ (absorbed)
        \item $w = \SI{-425}{J}$ (delivered to surroundings)
    \end{itemize}
    \begin{align*}
        \Delta E &= q + w \\
                 &= \SI{425}{J} + \SI{-425}{J} \\
                 &= \SI{425}{J} - \SI{425}{J} \\
                 &= \SI{0}{J}
    \end{align*}    

    Thus, the system's change in internal energy $\Delta E$ is \SI{0}{J}.
    \qed
\end{problem}
