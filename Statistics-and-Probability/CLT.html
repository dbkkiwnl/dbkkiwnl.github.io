Central Limit Theorem

The Central Limit Theorem states that the sampling distribution of the mean approaches a normal distribution, as the sample size increases. This means that regardless of the initial population distribution, the sampling distribution will approach the normal distribution as n gets larger.

The standard error of the mean or the standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the sampling mean, denoted as σx̄ is calculated by:

$$\sigma_{\bar{x}} = \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}} \tag{1}$$

where σ is the population standard deviation and n is the sample size.

Assume that the variable is normally distributed, the average time it takes a group of senior high school students to complete a certain examination is 46.2 minutes while the standard deviation is 8 minutes. What is the probability that a randomly selected senior high school student will complete the examination in less than 43 minutes? Does it seem reasonable that a senior high school student would finish the examination in less than 43 minutes?
Show solution
The following are given: μ = 46.2 minutes; σ = 8 minutes; X̅ = 43 minutes, and P(X̅ < 43) is missing. In order to find the correlating z-score: $$\begin{align*} z &= \frac{\bar{X} - \mu}{\sigma} \\ &= \frac{43 - 46.2}{8} \\ z &= 0.40 \end{align*}$$ Using the z-table, the corresponding value when z = 0.40 is 0.1554.
