The Definition of a Derivative

The derivative of a function \(f(x)\) is defined as: $$ f'(x) = \lim_{\Delta x \to 0} \frac{f(x + \Delta x) - f(x)}{\Delta x} \tag{1} $$

Note that there are (confusingly) multiple notations for derivatives. However, each one has their own benefit. In Lagrange notation, a prime mark (\('\)) denotes a derivative. For a function \(f\), its first derivative at \(x\) is written as \(f'(x)\).
